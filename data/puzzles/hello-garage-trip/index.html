The Cipher’s Walk

A key in hand, a string in tow,
we walk the path the symbols show.
Each letter turned, a shifting trace,
its form transformed, but kept in place.

The key unwinds with every round,
by twenty-six it’s wound and bound.
A quiet reminder, soft and sly,
tells how far each spin will fly.

A careful spin — not left to chance —
respects each letter’s native dance.
No change to those beyond the line,
just letters bent by key and time.

And when the trail at last is done,
the key lies faint, its power spun.
What’s left behind, a cryptic thread —
a string once locked, now softly read.

Midnight Garden

In midnight's garden, shadows grow,
Where moonlit petals softly glow.
A secret world in silver light,
Awakens gently in the night.

Autumn Leaves

Crimson leaves in swirling flight,
Dancing down from dizzy height.
They paint the earth in fiery hue,
A fleeting art the winds renew.

Reflections

Still waters hold the sky's embrace,
A mirror for the sun's warm face.
Ripples carry thoughts away,
To where the light and shadows play.

Morning Song

The dawn awakes with gentle song,
Inviting all to sing along.
A promise in the golden rays,
Of hope and joy for coming days.
